# Interpretive Design

EchoTrace Whispering Objects is built around a simple premise: visitors build stronger interpretations when they can assemble, test, and compare fragments together.

- **Constructivism**: Visitors connect sound, vibration, and light with partial narratives to build meaning.
- **Contextual learning**: Multilingual content and accessibility presets support different personal needs. Group discussion shapes shared interpretation.
- **Embodied cognition**: Proximity sensing, haptics, and light tie movement to attention and memory.
- **Inquiry and reconstruction**: Whisper fragments work like clues. The mystery object tests and reframes earlier assumptions.
- **Distributed learning**: No node holds the full story. Visitors need to circulate, compare, and synthesize.

## Visitor Journey

1. **Approach and discovery**: Visitors hear ambient whispers and move closer. LEDs indicate proximity.
2. **Embodied engagement**: Crossing the threshold triggers fuller audio, LED pulses, and optional haptics.
3. **Collaborative sense-making**: Visitors compare fragments and use transcripts to verify details.
4. **Mystery unlock**: Once enough fragments trigger, the mystery object plays the final segment.
5. **Reflection and re-entry**: Visitors often revisit nodes and transcripts to test new interpretations.

## Learning Goals

- Support curiosity-driven exploration of collections.
- Support collaborative storytelling where multiple perspectives are valid.
- Highlight the role of sensory evidence in constructing historical or cultural narratives.
- Encourage reflection on how access accommodations (captions, pacing, tactility) shift interpretive understanding.

These design choices make the installation participatory while keeping interpretation open-ended.
